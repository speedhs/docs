**6. Sales Process & Team Structure**

**Sales Funnel Stages:**

1.  **Awareness:** Digital ads, SEO, content, PR, events
2.  **Interest:** Website visits, virtual tours, content downloads
3.  **Consideration:** In-person tours, proposal requests, consultations
4.  **Intent:** Negotiation, contract review
5.  **Conversion:** Signed agreement, deposit paid
6.  **Onboarding:** Welcome process, community integration
7.  **Retention:** Ongoing engagement, renewal discussions
8.  **Advocacy:** Testimonials, referrals, case studies

**Sales Team Structure:**

-   **Head of Sales & Marketing:** Strategy, team leadership, key
    accounts
-   **Enterprise Sales Manager (1):** Focus on Segment 4 (Enterprise
    Solutions)
-   **SMB Sales Executives (2):** Focus on Segments 2 & 3 (Startups,
    SMBs)
-   **Community Sales Associate (1):** Focus on Segment 1 (Individuals),
    walk-ins, initial inquiries
-   **Marketing & Content Specialist (1):** Digital marketing, content
    creation, SEO
-   **Partnership Manager (1):** Broker relations, strategic alliances

**Sales Process Workflow:**

1.  Lead Generation (Marketing & Partnerships)
2.  Lead Qualification (Community Sales Associate / CRM Scoring)
3.  Initial Contact & Needs Assessment (Relevant Sales Exec)
4.  Virtual or In-Person Tour Scheduling
5.  Tour Conduction & Value Proposition Reinforcement
6.  Customized Proposal Generation
7.  Negotiation & Objection Handling
8.  Contract Finalization & Signing (Skelsa New Sales Approval Template)
9.  Deposit Collection & Onboarding Handoff (Skelsa Client Onboarding
    Form)

### New Sales Reporting Data Framework

**1. Objective**

To establish a standardized framework for reporting new sales data at
Skelsa, ensuring consistency, accuracy, and actionable insights for
strategic decision-making, performance tracking, and forecasting.

**2. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for New Sales**

**Lead Generation & Funnel Metrics:**

-   **Total Leads Generated:** Number of new leads acquired (by source:
    Website, Social, Referral, Event, Broker, Walk-in, Other).
-   **Lead Quality Score:** Average score of leads based on predefined
    criteria (e.g., BANT, ICP fit).
-   **Lead Conversion Rate (Stage-to-Stage):**
    -   Lead to Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL) %
    -   MQL to Sales Qualified Lead (SQL) %
    -   SQL to Opportunity %
    -   Opportunity to Tour/Demo %
    -   Tour/Demo to Proposal %
    -   Proposal to Closed-Won %
-   **Overall Lead-to-Win Rate:** Percentage of total leads that convert
    to paying members.
-   **Sales Cycle Length:** Average time from lead creation to
    closed-won deal (in days).
-   **Funnel Velocity:** Average time spent in each sales funnel stage.

**Revenue & Deal Metrics:**

-   **New Monthly Recurring Revenue (New MRR):** Total MRR generated
    from new sales in the reporting period.
-   **Average Revenue Per New Account (ARPA):** Average MRR per new
    member/company.
-   **Average Revenue Per Seat (ARPS) - New Sales:** Average MRR per new
    seat sold.
-   **Total Contract Value (TCV) - New Sales:** Sum of the total value of
    all new contracts signed.
-   **Average Contract Length (ACL) - New Sales:** Average duration of
    new contracts signed (in months).
-   **New Bookings Value:** Total value of new deals closed (can be TCV
    or first-year ACV).
-   **Discount Rate %:** Average discount provided on new deals.
-   **Product Mix:** Breakdown of new sales by membership type (Hot Desk,
    Dedicated, Private Office sizes, Enterprise).

**Sales Activity & Efficiency Metrics:**

-   **Number of Tours/Demos Conducted:** Total tours or demos completed.
-   **Number of Proposals Sent:** Total proposals delivered to prospects.
-   **Sales Activity per Rep:** Calls, emails, meetings per sales
    representative.
-   **Quota Attainment %:** Percentage of sales target achieved by
    individual reps and the team.
-   **Win Rate by Sales Rep:** Individual sales rep close rates.
-   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) - New Sales:** Total sales and
    marketing spend for new acquisitions divided by the number of new
    customers acquired.

**3. Data Sources**

-   **CRM System (e.g., HubSpot, Salesforce):** Primary source for lead
    information, deal stages, sales activities, customer interactions,
    and contract details.
-   **Website Analytics (e.g., Google Analytics):** Lead source tracking,
    website form submissions, campaign performance.
-   **Marketing Automation Platform (e.g., Mailchimp, Marketo):** Email
    campaign performance, lead nurturing data.
-   **Billing System (e.g., Zoho Books, Chargebee):** Confirmed MRR,
    payment status, contract start/end dates.
-   **Sales Team Manual Input:** Call logs, meeting notes, specific deal
    insights (to be minimized through CRM integration).
-   **Customer Feedback Platforms:** Post-tour surveys, onboarding
    feedback.

**4. Reporting Structure & Frequency**

**Daily Reports (Sales Manager, Sales Reps):**

-   **Focus:** Real-time activity and immediate pipeline health.
-   **Metrics:**
    -   New Leads Generated (today)
    -   Tours Scheduled/Completed (today)
    -   Proposals Sent (today)
    -   Deals Closed-Won (today)
    -   Key Activities Logged (calls, emails, meetings)
-   **Format:** CRM Dashboard, automated email summary.

**Weekly Reports (Sales Team, Head of Sales):**

-   **Focus:** Short-term performance, pipeline progression, and rep
    activity.
-   **Metrics:**
    -   Weekly Lead Generation by Source
    -   Funnel Conversions (Lead to MQL, MQL to SQL, etc.)
    -   Tours Completed vs. Goal
    -   Proposals Sent vs. Goal
    -   New MRR Booked (week-to-date)
    -   Pipeline Value by Stage
    -   Top Performing Lead Sources
    -   Individual Rep Activity Summary
-   **Format:** Standardized report template, sales team meeting
    presentation.

**Monthly Reports (Head of Sales, Executive Team):**

-   **Focus:** Overall sales performance against targets, trend analysis,
    and strategic insights.
-   **Metrics:**
    -   Total New MRR vs. Target
    -   ARPA and ARPS Trends
    -   Overall Lead-to-Win Rate
    -   Average Sales Cycle Length
    -   CAC for the month
    -   Product Mix Performance
    -   Sales Team Quota Attainment
    -   Win/Loss Analysis (reasons for lost deals)
    -   Pipeline Coverage Ratio
-   **Format:** Comprehensive sales performance report, executive summary
    dashboard.

**Quarterly Reports (Executive Team, Investors):**

-   **Focus:** Long-term trends, strategic impact, and forecasting.
-   **Metrics:**
    -   Quarterly New MRR vs. Target and YoY Growth
    -   LTV of New Customers Acquired
    -   LTV:CAC Ratio
    -   Market Segment Performance (revenue and growth by segment)
    -   Sales Channel Effectiveness (ROI per channel)
    -   Updated Sales Forecast for next quarter/year
    -   Key Learnings and Strategic Adjustments
-   **Format:** Detailed presentation, investor update deck section.

**5. Data Collection & Validation Process**

1.  **Standardized Data Entry:** All sales reps must follow consistent
    data entry protocols in the CRM for leads, contacts, companies, and
    deals.
2.  **Automated Data Capture:** Maximize automation for lead capture from
    website, email, and other digital channels.
3.  **Regular Data Cleansing:** Implement a weekly process to identify
    and correct inaccuracies, duplicates, or incomplete data in the CRM.
4.  **Cross-System Reconciliation:** Monthly reconciliation of CRM data
    with the billing system to ensure MRR and contract accuracy.
5.  **Sales Manager Review:** Sales managers are responsible for
    reviewing their team\s pipeline data weekly for accuracy and
    completeness.
6.  **Automated Alerts:** Set up alerts for missing critical data fields
    or deals stalled in a stage for too long.

**6. Report Generation & Distribution**

-   **Automated Reporting:** Utilize CRM reporting features and BI tools
    (e.g., Tableau, Google Data Studio) to automate report generation
    wherever possible.
-   **Standardized Templates:** Use consistent templates for all reports
    to ensure clarity and ease of comparison over time.
-   **Centralized Repository:** Store all sales reports in a shared,
    accessible location (e.g., company intranet, shared drive).
-   **Scheduled Distribution:** Reports should be generated and
    distributed on a predefined schedule.
-   **Access Control:** Ensure appropriate access levels for different
    reports based on roles.

**7. Continuous Improvement**

-   **Regular Review Meetings:** Conduct weekly sales meetings and monthly
    performance reviews to discuss reports, identify trends, and agree
    on actions.
-   **Feedback Mechanism:** Establish a process for the sales team and
    other stakeholders to provide feedback on the reporting framework.
-   **KPI Review:** Annually review and update KPIs to ensure they remain
    relevant to business objectives.
-   **Technology Updates:** Continuously explore and implement new tools
    or CRM features that can improve sales reporting and analytics.

This framework will provide Skelsa with a clear, consistent, and
insightful view of its new sales performance, enabling data-driven
decisions and sustainable growth.

### Skelsa Monthly Churn Reporting Dashboard

**1. Dashboard Objective**

To provide a clear, concise, and actionable monthly overview of member
churn at Skelsa. This dashboard will help identify trends, understand
reasons for churn, and inform retention strategies.

**2. Key Churn Metrics (to be displayed prominently)**

-   **Monthly Churn Rate (%):** (Number of Churned Members in Month /
    Total Members at Start of Month) \* 100
-   **Net MRR Churn Rate (%):** ((MRR Lost from Churned Members - MRR
    Gained from Upgrades) / Total MRR at Start of Month) \* 100
-   **Gross MRR Churn Rate (%):** (MRR Lost from Churned Members / Total
    MRR at Start of Month) \* 100
-   **Number of Churned Members (Count):** Absolute number of members who
    terminated their membership.
-   **MRR Lost from Churn (Value):** Total MRR value lost due to churned
    members.
-   **Average Member Lifetime (Months/Years):** For the churned cohort.
-   **Customer Retention Rate (%):** 100% - Monthly Churn Rate %

**3. Data Visualization & Sections**

**Section 1: Headline Churn Metrics (Current Month vs. Previous Month
& Target)**

-   Display the key churn metrics listed above with clear visual
    indicators for performance against the previous month and targets
    (e.g., green for improvement, red for decline).

-   **Line Chart:** Net MRR Churn Rate (%) over the last 12 months.

    months.
